    300 people 299 Likes

    The macaron originated in Italy during the Renaissance and was introduced to France by Catherine de' Medici. It evolved into its modern form in the 18th century, gaining popularity in Parisian bakeries. Pierre Desfontaines is credited with creating the sandwiched macaron with ganache filling. Today, macarons are enjoyed worldwide as a symbol of French culinary elegance.
